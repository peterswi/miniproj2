Visualizations: Mini Project 2

Erin Corcoran, Lurein Perera, Will Peters & Kathryn Tatum

For our project, we decided to explore data on fatalities in police shootings in the United States since 2015. This data comes from the Washington Post's database on police shootings.

This is analysis of police killings in the US since 2015.

First, we wanted to know how many people were killed by police, and who they were. With this focus on the "who," we examined a variety of demographic data: age, gender, and race.
Our questions will appear above the visualizations that provide insight to them.

  • How many people have been killed by police since 2015?
  • Are these fatalities specific to a particlar region?
All People Killed
  • Are there any patterns in the ages of those killed?
  • Are men and women killed at the same rate?
  • What is the racial breakdown of those killed? Does it match that of the US?
